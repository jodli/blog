## Why this exists

I've been writing notes for years — in Obsidian, in meeting docs, in random text files. Half-formed thoughts about software, AI, and how we build things. Most of them never leave my vault.

This blog is an attempt to **think out loud**. Not polished essays, not hot takes. Just: here's what I noticed, here's what I tried, here's what I think about it.

## What I've been thinking about

I've spent the last months running AI agents on real codebases, building small tools for people around me, and watching an organization try (and fail) to modernize a legacy product three times in a row. Some things I keep coming back to:

- Everyone's talking about AI making developers 10x faster. But coding was never the bottleneck. Decisions were. Alignment was. Building the wrong thing was. Making the coding part faster doesn't fix any of that.
- I ran three AI agents in parallel across three repos. It was surprisingly useful — and surprisingly broken in ways nobody talks about.
- I build software for my family. Invoicing for my partner's yoga classes, utility cost tracking for our house, a diabetes data pipeline for my kid. When the user sits at your dinner table, you learn things about software that no sprint review will teach you.
- Architecture decisions that should take a day somehow take seven weeks. Not because the problem is hard, but because the organization is the bottleneck.

Some of this will turn into posts. Some won't. No schedule, no promises.

Let's see where this goes.
